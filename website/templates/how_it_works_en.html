    Como Usar o Portal

    Como Mudar Moedas (Avançado)

    When you exchange money on the Portal you don't trade with an intermediary. Instead you trade directly with other users of the platform. Currently, the Portal has only one currency market, between Euros (EUR) and Dobras (STN).

    You are able to place an offer in the market at any time, either to buy Euros using Dobras or to sell Euros in exchange for Dobras. You simply specify the amount that you would like to buy or sell and your reserve price. Then your orders are matched with the best available orders from the oppersite side. Let's look at some examples now:

    Quant. Preço Preço Quant.
    8.00 26.00 26.10 5.00
    15.00 25.80 26.50 30.00
    25.00 25.00 - -

    On the left of this market, in green, we can see the orders seeking to buy Euros. There is one person who wants to buy 25 Euros for 25.00 Dobras each, one person who wants to buy 15 Euros for 25.80 Dobras each and one person who wants to buy 8 Euros 26.00 Dobras each. Those who are offering to buy for the best (highest) prices are listed first and they will be able to trade ahead of those offering lower prices.

    On the right of this market, in red, we can see the orders seeking to sell Euros. There is one person who wants to sell 5 Euros for 26.10 Dobras each and one person who wants to buy 30 Euros for 26.5 Dobras each. This time, those who are demanding to sell for the best (lowest) prices are listed first and they will be able to trade before people demanding higher prices.

    Now let's imagine that you want to buy Euros and so you place an order to purchase 10 Euros for 27.00 Dobras each. This price is higher than the price of the first seller so you trade instantly buying 5 Euros for 26.10 Dobras each. At this point you still want to buy 5 more Euros so you will also match with the second seller buying 5 Euros from them for 26.50 Dobras each. So in total you will pay 263.5 Dobras even though you were willing to pay up to 270 Dobras (27 × 10). After your trade the market will look like this:

    10.00

    27.00

    Quant. Preço Preço Quant.
    8.00 26.00 26.50 25.00
    15.00 25.80 - -
    25.00 25.00 - -
    - - - -

    In this next example let's imagine that you want you still want to buy 10 Euros but this time you specify a maximum price of 26.20 Dobras. Like before your price is higher than the price of the first seller so you match with them, buying 5 Euros for 26.10 Dobras each. However, this time you are not willing to pay enough to trade with the second seller. So the rest of your order will remain in the market until someone agrees to sell at 26.20 Dobras or until you cancel the order. This is how the market looks after your trade:

    10.00

    26.20

    Quant. Preço Preço Quant.
    5.00 26.20 26.50 30.00
    8.00 26.00 - -
    15.00 25.80 - -
    25.00 25.00 - -

    Since your order is now the first buyer you probably won't have to wait long until someone trades with you. Once you trade you will have paid 261.5 Dobras for your 10 Euros.

    Finally, let's suppose that you still want to buy 10 Euros but you are very patient and really want to trade at a favourable (low) price. One strategy is to insert an order to buy for 25.01 Dobras. This order will not trade immediately because there are not currently any sellers willing to accept a price that low. Instead, the order will sit in the market as you can see here:

    10.00

    25.01

    Quant. Preço Preço Quant.
    8.00 26.00 26.10 5.00
    15.00 25.80 26.50 30.00
    10.00 25.01 - -
    25.00 25.00 - -

    Your order is currently third in the queue. However, if someone places a sell order larger than 23 Euros (8 + 15) and their limit is low enough then at least part of your order will trade. It is perfectly alright for only some of your order to trade and for the rest to trade with someone eles later on. Note that by offering 0.01 Dobras more than the last buyer you are able to rank ahead of them in the queue. If you had only offered 24.99 Dobras then you would only trade if the opposing order was larger than 48 Euros (8 + 15 + 25).

    A good rule of thumb to adopt is that if you need to move money though the Portal quickly, especially if it is a large amount of money, you may have to do so at an unfavourable exchange rate. However, if you are able to wait for others to come to you and/or move the money in smaller installments then you are likely to get much better prices.

    An order will remain active in the market until it is cancelled or traded, even if you logout of your account or turn off your device. If you change your mind then you can cancel any order that hasn't traded yet by simply clicking the × icon next to that order.

    Orders can be inserted by users of the Portal at any time. Remember to refresh you webpage to get the most current shapshot of the market.
